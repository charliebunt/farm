General Information:

231 hectares of farmland approximately 700 metres above sea level and 45 kilometres from the Southern edge of Canberra.

"Ingelara" sloping east from a high background of hills and mountains flattens out and stops at the Monaro Highway.

The Ingelara creek running through the Northern end of the farm defines part of the boundary and spreads out into a large dam.

North of the dam there is a neck of grazing land that climbs the hill and opens out into a sheltered paddock.

A converted dairy "Ingelara" has a license to irrigate eighty one hectares of land and an underground system of water pipes mainly on the flat. A pump on the dam wall can pump water to the flat or up to the orchard in the Southern paddock.

Besides the main residence, three cottages are spread out beside the dam or creek and the sheds are clustered together near the main dam.

The local average rainfall is 24" per year and comes mainly from the South or North West.

As Ingelara is almost surrounded by hills or mountains the dam fills up from many directions when it rains heavily.
